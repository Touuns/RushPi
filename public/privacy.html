Privacy Policy

Rush Pi — casual daily runner game · MVP / Testnet phase

Rush Pi is a casual runner game. This policy explains, in plain terms, what data the app uses. Rush Pi is currently in a testing / MVP phase.

Pi Network authentication

The app may use Pi Network authentication to display your Pi username inside the game. This is optional — the game is fully playable without connecting a Pi account.

Payments

  • The app may process a small Testnet User-to-App payment only when you explicitly tap the test payment button.
  • No gameplay advantage is granted through any payment.
  • The app does not ask for, store, or access your wallet passphrase.

Data stored on your device

The app stores gameplay data locally in your browser's localStorage, including:

  • scores and best score
  • XP, level and badges
  • local leaderboard entries
  • settings / progress
  • a local flag when the Pi test payment is completed

No sensitive personal data is intentionally collected. This data stays in your browser.

Server leaderboard

If you are connected with Pi and you complete a Daily Run, that run's result is sent to our server (Supabase) to power the global leaderboard. The data sent is:

  • your Pi username and Pi user id
  • that run's stats: score, energies collected, max combo, obstacles hit, and run duration

Training scores are never sent. If you do not connect Pi, no score is sent to the server. We do not send or store your wallet passphrase, payment details, wallet address, IP address, or location.

Clearing your data

You can clear locally stored data at any time using the app's "Reset local data" option, or through your browser's storage settings.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact the developer through the Pi app listing or the project repository.

These pages are simple and do not constitute professional legal advice. They provide a clear baseline for an MVP / Testnet application.
